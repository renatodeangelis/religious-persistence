# Religious Persistence

This project analyzes trends in religious persistence, switching, and disaffiliation in the United States using data from the General Social Survey (GSS).

## Data

The analysis uses the GSS cumulative cross-sectional survey (1972–2024), which asks respondents about their current religious affiliation and the religion they were raised in. Key variables include:

- Current and childhood religious affiliation (`relig`, `relig16`) and denomination (`denom`, `denom16`)
- Strength of religious identity (`reliten`)
- Spousal religion, education, birth cohort, and survey year

## Main Question

To what extent do Americans stay in the religion they were raised in — and how has this changed over time and across birth cohorts? The project examines persistence and defection across major religious traditions (Catholic, Mainline Protestant, Conservative Protestant, and None), tracking both the share who remain and the strength of their affiliation among those who do.
